# Writer profiles

φ can be as bare as a single page or as full as a whole writing studio — and you
choose which, per vault, with a **writer profile**. A profile decides *which
features are shown*. It never changes what φ can do and never touches your work;
hiding a feature simply tidies it out of the way.

## The two built-in profiles

- **Simple** — just you and the page. The core editor and formatting, collections,
  find & replace, templates, and the session timer. The heavier machinery stays
  out of sight.
- **Full** — everything Simple has, plus the tools for larger and more scholarly
  work: the graph view, world-building (Characters, Authors, Research), citations
  & bibliography, readability stats, the table-of-contents block, the Journal, and
  Morning Pages.

New vaults start on **Full**.

## "How do you want φ to feel?"

The first time you open a vault, φ asks one question — **How do you want φ to
feel?** — with two answers:

- **Full — every tool available**
- **Simple — just you and the page**

Pick one, or **Skip** to keep the default (Full). It's framed as *how much you
want on screen*, not as a skill level, and you can change it any time.

## What a profile hides — and never deletes

Turning a feature off hides its **entry points**: its rail icon, menu items, and
slash commands. It never removes anything.

- The feature's data stays exactly where it is. Hide the Journal and your entries
  are still there; turn it back on and they reappear, untouched.
- A hidden feature is still reachable from the **command palette** (`⌘P`), so
  nothing is ever locked away — it's only tidied off the main surface.

The features a profile can show or hide:

- **Graph view**
- **World-building** — the Characters, Authors, and Research rail entries
- **Citations & bibliography**
- **Readability stats** (in the document-stats popover)
- **Table of contents** (the slash block)
- **Journal**
- **Morning Pages**
- **Session timer**
- **Templates**

## Managing profiles — Settings → Profiles

Open **Settings** (`⌘,`) → **Profiles**. You'll see **Simple**, **Full**, and any
custom profiles you've made. For each one you can:

- **Use in this vault** — apply it to the vault you're in.
- **Use in another vault…** — apply it to a different vault.
- **Set as global default** — the profile a vault uses when it hasn't chosen its
  own.
- **Duplicate** — copy it as a starting point for a custom profile.
- **Rename** / **Delete** — for your own custom profiles. The built-in Simple and
  Full can't be renamed or deleted.

Below the list, the **Features** grid toggles each feature for the active profile,
and you set the **Streak display** and **Weekly rhythm** here too — see
[The calendar](calendar.md).

### Custom profiles

Change any toggle while a built-in is active and φ quietly makes an **editable
copy** for you rather than altering the built-in — so Simple and Full always stay
as they are. Give the copy a name and it becomes a reusable profile you can apply
to other vaults or set as your global default.

Profiles are remembered **per vault**, so a novel and a journal can each surface
just what they need. A vault that hasn't pinned its own profile follows the
**global default**.
